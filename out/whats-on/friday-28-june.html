Friday 28 June

Image credit: Guy Milnes

A cultural feast for film and spoken word enthusiasts today with a special Spoken Word night at Antenna Café Thing and open air cinema at Coopers Yard. The cinema features a rare showing of The Pleasure Garden shot in Crystal Palace park in 1953.

Other events include a reminiscence morning at the library, bingo and a tea dance moving on into the evening with lots of retro entertainment. Westow House’s 1950s extravaganza will transport you back to the rock ‘n’ roll era while you can dance the night away to some of the best classic songs at The White Hart. For something different check out the Guinness and oyster fest at the Gipsy Hill Tavern – see the full programme here.

In the daytime

  • Remembering the Crystal Palace

    10:30 @ Upper Norwood Methodist Church

    Fiona Byers from Upper Norwood Library hosts a reminiscence session based on the legacy of the Crystal Palace. Bring your family photographs and souvenirs of the Palace and the park to trigger memories and swap stories.

  • Bingo for everyone

    14:00 @ Living Water Satisfies

    Prize bingo with refreshments, £2 on the door.

  • Tea dance

    17:30 @ Upper Norwood Methodist Church Hall

    Lose yourself for the afternoon with music spun on an original gramophone, also with pianist Alison Chai. It's free and subsidised refreshments are available.

See full programme

In the evening

  • DJ retro night

    19:00 @ The White Hart

    With some of the best classic songs of all time.

  • The Overground Special Festival Spoken Word

    19:30 @ Antenna Studios

    The very popular Spoken Word Nights in Antenna take it up a few notches for the festival with an absolutely electric line-up of performers featuring El Crisis+ David J Pugilist+Tamsin Kendrick+ Jazzman John Clarke+K L Gillespie +Zuli Stenson+Open Mic. Hosted by Joe Duggan. Free admission. (Photo by John Barret)

  • Guinness and Oyster Festival

    19:30 @ Gipsy Hill Tavern

    Fresh Irish oysters served up by the Oyster Meister, featuring live music from Irish folk rockers The Reels plus DJ Mighty Craic.

  • 1950s rock’n’roll extravaganza

    20:00 @ Westow House

    Friday night Westow House will be transformed into a 1950s rock ‘n’ roll bar - live performances from the Boom Boom Booms, genuine 1950s jukebox, swing/jive dance lessons & and much more!

  • The Electric Palace open air cinema

    20:30 @ Coopers Yard

    Opening the programme is The Library (2012) an animated short by Leo Crane and following this is a rare showing of The Pleasure Garden (1953), a BBC short film, on its 60th anniversary. This special event will be followed by BAFTA-winning short, A Morning Stroll, from local film-maker Grant Orchard and then the main feature Little Miss Sunshine. With bar and music – tickets £7.50 – click below to pay via PayPal (includes free drink on arrival) Now sold out!

  • DJ club classics

    21:00 @ Twenty-Five

    DJ playing commercial and club classics till 2am
