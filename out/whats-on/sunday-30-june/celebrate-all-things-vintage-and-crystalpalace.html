Celebrate all things vintage and Crystal Palace!

At festival HQ we thought it was about time the lively and growing vintage and antiques culture in Crystal Palace had its own event so here it is!

Get yourselves down to the cul de sac of St Aubyn’s Road and Church Road in your best vintage gear for an afternoon of music, food and shopping with our best vintage clothing traders, food and music from the best in live bands and DJs! From 12pm to 6pm.

Food from Shakey’s Diner – American diner themed food - and the vintage ice-cream van.

From 12pm to 6pm

  • The Boom Boom Booms

    Go check 'em out!

    Four singers, big double bass, two screaming gretsch guitars, and one hot sax are the bedrock sound of a truly international band of miscreants with personnel hailing from Liverpool, London, Toronto, and New York City. Meeting in the depths of an East End rockabilly strip club the Booms formed and started rocking London venues within a few weeks. They quickly gained a weekly residency at What the Jive in Highbury before forming their own monthly event Aliens Ate My Cadillac in Shoreditch.

  • The Obscuritones

    Listen to The Obscuritones

    Close harmony and rockin’ rhythm. Like the Andrews Sisters singin’ with the Stray Cats after a night out with the Cramps.

  • Harriet Eaves

    Listen to Harriet Eaves

    Harriet Eaves is a freelance jazz singer based in South London. She has perfomed in some of the UK’s top venues, including the M.E.N. Arena, Royal Albert Hall, Cardiff Millennium Centre, Brixton O2 Academy and the Glasgow Royal Concert Hall. In October/November 2012 and April 2013 she toured as a backing singer and featured soloist for James, performing to audiences of up to 23,000. Recently, Harriet has also performed with Peter Hook (Joy Division), Tim Burgess (The Charlatans) and Puressence at Manchester Cathedral as a part of 'Cathedral for Cities'.

    During her time at Manchester University, Harriet enjoyed multiple appearances as guest soloist with The University of Manchester Big Band, performing in Manchester’s Club Academy and at The Lost Theatre, London. Harriet has also performed at numerous events around the UK, singing with function bands as well as with solo piano. She recently recorded her new EP which can be sampled at www.harrieteaves.co.uk
