Festival fun in the Triangle

The very first Triangle day launches this year so dust yourself off and come back out for more fun! As well as the vintage hub and the fine art and craft market, our traders, churches and pubs host a ton of events to continue the festival and round off the weekend!

Triangle Day

  • Papagaio’s Pirate Party

    11:00 @ 83 Church Road

    Drop in all day up to 3pm with kids’ craft table and the Name our Parrot competition

  • Victory Place arts and fitness

    12:00 @ CrossFit Performance 360 London

    A festival within the festival with demonstrations, performances and more. Ballet, Zumba, street dance, CrossFit, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, Calisthentics and boxing with BBQ, bouncy castle and music

  • Dulwich Ukulele band

    12:00 @ The Alma

    BBQ and live music from Dulwich Ukulele band plus guests until late

  • The White Hart’s session in the recession

    12:00 @ The White Hart

    Longfellow and other bands – all day in the front garden

  • Café Sessions Alight Here special

    12:00 @ Antenna Studios

    Stay floating on the festival vibes with a BBQ and luminous live music from Breezy Lee & The Spaceshipman, The Lovebirds, The No Frills Band, Franck Alba, Dani Cali, Nina Walsh and more TBA. Limited capacity, arrive early to avoid disappointment

  • Grooveschool

    12:00 @ Antenna Studios' car park, Haynes Lane

    Don’t miss out on our very special DJ and basic music technology workshops for young people aged 8-19 – open until 6pm.

  • Westow House's Triangle day

    12:00 @ Antenna Studios’ car park, Haynes Lane

    The garden fun continues to help celebrate the first ever Triangle Day with yet another hog roast, BBQ and outdoor cider bar with buskers and entertainers to captivate.

  • BBQ in the garden

    12:00 @ Twenty-Five

  • The Grape & Grain live music

    14:00 @ The Grape & Grain

    The fantastic Parry Ray & John Crawford, playing the coolest, laid back lounge jazz followed by The Sax Pastilles at 5pm, a six piece version of our now legendary house band, putting the swing into swing/jazz.

  • DJ sessions

    15:00 @ The Exhibition Rooms

    Smooth out those festival rough edges downstairs at The Exhibition Rooms with cocktails and a selection of eclectic beats from local DJ The Wilkybar Kid, ranging from chilled electronic, ska and soul, to disco and downtempo house, followed by your favourite resident DJs playing rare vinyl, Brazilian specialities and own compositions from 7pm onwards.

  • Live music at The Royal Albert

    16:00 @ The Royal Albert

  • Ron McElroy

    16:00 @ Bambino's, Church Road

    This is Ron’s first time performing at the festival, with lifelong friend and musical partner Jim Cartwright on drums. Jim is a natural, he makes playing drums look like slamming a door or dropping a cup – the easiest thing in the world. With an emphasis on dynamics, soul-style energy and rhythm you will not see them waste a note. Together they are 'Stronger than a forged crank, Sweeter than a sugar mouse.'

  • Local history walk with the Norwood Society

    16:15 @ Starts at Crystal Palace station

    A 90 minute walk around the Church Road/Fox Hill area looking at some of the houses and buildings, and the lives of the people who lived in them.

  • Beer garden BBQ, jazz, cajun and blues

    18:00 @ Gipsy Hill Tavern

    Dakota Jim and his family band plus DJ.

  • Spectrum Ensemble London

    19:30 @ St. John the Evangelist Church, Auckland Road

    An evening of pieces from the string, piano and symphonic repertoire as you've never heard them before, newly arranged for wind and brass ensemble. Shostakovich, Debussy, Sibelius, Ravel, Holst, Mahler and more all in one concert! Tickets £5 pay on the door, under 16s free. Spectrum Ensemble London was formed in 2012 with the specific aim of creating new music for and wind and brass ensemble. The instruments of the group have been deliberately chosen for their individual sonority which when combined form a uniquely rich, dark sound palette. The players, most local to south east London, are all professional and their collective CVs make for impressive reading.
