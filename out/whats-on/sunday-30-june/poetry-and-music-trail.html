Poetry and music trail

Brought to you by the festival team and local traders, some very special performers from our Sound Tracks concert and others come to the Triangle.

Can’t leave the Triangle? Follow the trail for a magical afternoon of music and poetry in some of our best-loved shops:

Vintage hub, Church Road and St Aubyn’s Road cul de sac

12:00 Crystal Palace Community Choir
A home-grown choir that welcomes all and sings everything!

Crystal Palace Antiques, Jasper Road

12:15 Favourite and new performers from Beyond Words
Your local regular literary and musical spinners of magic present Matthew Caley and Orlanda Marsden

13:00 Brother G and The Trouble
Experimental music and sound with spoken word using piano, reel to reel tape machines, accordion, clarinet, percussion and the voice.

Kaleidoscope fine art and craft fair, Haynes Lane

13:30 Crystal Palace Community Choir
A home-grown choir that welcomes all and sings everything!

Blue Door Bicycles, Central Hill

14:00 Tim Bridgen – vibraphone
Percussionist Tim will transport listeners to the vibe of a late night jazz club, playing an eclectic mix of music from J.S Bach to Joe Locke

14:45 Favourite and new performers from Beyond Words
– Your local regular literary and musical spinners of magic present Joe Cullen and Rosanna Raymond

The Secret Garden, Coxwell Road

14:45 Crystal Palace Community Choir
A home-grown choir that welcomes all and sings everything!

15:30 Keziah Thomas – harp
Award-winning local harpist presents music first performed at the Crystal Palace by John Thomas and Arthur Sullivan

16:15 Abigail Sudbury – soprano
Continuing the theme of music at the Crystal Palace, songs by Arthur Sullivan, Ivor Novello and August Manns accompanied by the harp

17:00 Favourite and new performers from Beyond Words
Your local regular literary and musical spinners of magic present Mars Willis - story-teller and musician and Sophie Herxheimer – poet and artist.
