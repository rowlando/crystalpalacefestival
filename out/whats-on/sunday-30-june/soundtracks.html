Soundtracks – classical and resonant music at Crystal Palace station

Experience the incredible acoustics of our beautiful station and vibrant performances from local classical and acoustic musicians, with a special brunch menu at Brown & Green in the station.

Morning

  • Matthew McCarthy – clarinet quartet

    10:15

    S.P.A.M. music from the past 500 years, written or arranged for four clarinets

  • Keziah Thomas – harp

    11:00

    Award-winning local harpist presents music first performed at the Crystal Palace by John Thomas and Arthur Sullivan

  • Tim Bridgen – vibraphone

    11:30

    Percussionist Tim will transport listeners to the vibe of a late night jazz club, playing an eclectic mix of music from J.S Bach to Joe Locke

See full programme

Afternoon

  • Elizabeth Hungerford – soprano

    12:15

    Early music specialist performs works by Handel, Purcell and more

  • Abigail Sudbury – soprano

    13:00

    Continuing the theme of music at the Crystal Palace, songs by Arthur Sullivan, Ivor Novello and August Manns accompanied by the harp

  • Ensemble Tramontana

    13:45

    Music that crosses medieval and Renaissance Europe, stirring the emotions, focusing on themes of travel, madness, and love

  • Kate Proudlove – soprano and piano

    14:30

    Songs capturing the spirit of England accompanied by Charlotte Way on the piano

  • Brother G and The Trouble

    14:30

    Experimental music and sound with spoken word using piano, reel to reel tape machines, accordion, clarinet, percussion and the voice.
